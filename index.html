Network Throughput
Throughput measures actual data transferred per second. It is always lower than theoretical capacity due to overhead, congestion, and retransmissions, and is expressed in Mbps.
Typical Range: 10–1000 Mbps
Packet Transmission Theory
Data is broken into packets. Each travels independently and is reassembled at the destination. TCP guarantees delivery; UDP prioritises speed — ideal for streaming and VoIP.
MTU: 1500 bytes (Ethernet)
Signal Latency
Latency is the round-trip time for a signal. Sub-20ms is excellent; 20–100ms is good for most apps; above 150ms causes noticeable lag in interactive sessions.
Target: < 20ms (optimal)
Network Health Score
A composite score derived from packet loss, jitter, latency stability, and throughput consistency. Above 90 indicates enterprise-grade reliability.
Score > 90: Enterprise Grade
